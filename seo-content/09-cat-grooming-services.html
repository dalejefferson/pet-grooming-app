Here's a number that should get your attention: 47 million households in the United States own at least one cat. That's more than dog-owning households.

Yet walk into most grooming salons and ask about cat services. You'll likely get a hesitant look, a mumbled "we don't really do cats," or at best, a basic bath-and-brush offering.

This massive gap between cat ownership and cat grooming services represents one of the biggest untapped opportunities in the pet industry.

If you're looking to grow your cat grooming services business, you're about to discover why feline clients could be your most profitable niche.

The Untapped Opportunity in Cat Grooming

Most groomers avoid cats for understandable reasons. Cats are different from dogs. They can be unpredictable. They require different handling techniques.

But here's what those groomers are missing: that difficulty creates scarcity, and scarcity creates opportunity.

When you're one of the few groomers in your area who confidently handles cats, you become the go-to recommendation. Cat owners share information with other cat owners. One skilled feline groomer can dominate an entire local market.

The Math Works: If you add just 10 cat clients per month at an average of $85 per visit, that's $850 in additional monthly revenue, or over $10,000 annually. And cat owners tend to be loyal once they find a groomer who handles their pet well.

Why Cat Owners Desperately Need Professional Grooming

There's a persistent myth that cats groom themselves and don't need professional care. This myth costs cat owners and their pets dearly.

The Reality of Cat Grooming Needs

Long-haired cats frequently develop mats that become painful if left untreated. Persian, Maine Coon, and Ragdoll owners often struggle with daily maintenance.

Senior cats lose flexibility and can no longer groom themselves properly. Many develop matted backs and rear ends because they simply can't reach those areas anymore.

Overweight cats face similar challenges. They can't twist and turn to maintain their coats, leading to hygiene issues and discomfort.

Cats with health conditions may have oily or flaky skin that requires professional treatment and specialized products.

Shedding management is a year-round concern. Professional de-shedding reduces hairballs and keeps homes cleaner.

Common Problems You'll Solve

  • Severe matting: Often requiring shave-downs that owners can't do themselves
  • Nail overgrowth: Cat nails can grow into paw pads, causing pain and infection
  • Hygiene issues: Particularly in long-haired cats, the rear area needs regular maintenance
  • Excessive shedding: Professional de-shedding dramatically reduces loose fur
  • Skin conditions: Medicated baths and treatments for various dermatological issues

Essential Cat Grooming Techniques

Cat grooming is not dog grooming with a smaller animal. Success requires understanding feline psychology and physiology.

The Mindset Shift

Dogs generally want to please. Cats want to feel safe. Your entire approach must center on creating a calm, secure environment.

Speed isn't the goal with cats. Patience and gentle handling produce better results, less stress, and, ironically, often faster overall sessions as cats become cooperative rather than combative.

Handling Techniques That Work

  • Scruffing debate: Modern feline handling has moved away from heavy scruffing. Light scruff support is acceptable; full suspension is not
  • Towel wrapping: A properly wrapped towel can calm cats and protect you while allowing access to one area at a time
  • Minimal restraint: Less is more. Over-restraining triggers fight-or-flight responses
  • Reading body language: Ear position, tail movement, and vocalizations all communicate stress levels
  • Strategic breaks: Sometimes the best technique is stopping, letting the cat reset, and continuing
Safety First: Never attempt to groom an aggressive or extremely fearful cat without proper training. Some cats require veterinary sedation for grooming. Knowing when to refer to a vet is part of professional cat grooming.

Core Services to Offer

Basic Bath and Brush

Full bath with cat-appropriate shampoo, blow dry, brush out, nail trim, ear cleaning. The foundation service for maintenance clients.

Typical pricing: $65-95

De-matting and De-shedding

Intensive coat work for cats with mats or heavy shedding. May require extended time depending on severity.

Typical pricing: $85-150

Lion Cut

Full body shave leaving mane, boots, and tail pom. Popular for severely matted cats and owners who want low-maintenance coats.

Typical pricing: $95-150

Sanitary Trim

Trimming the rear area for hygiene. Quick service that many long-haired cat owners need regularly.

Typical pricing: $25-45

Nail Trim Only

Quick service for cats that only need nail maintenance. Can be a gateway service to full grooming.

Typical pricing: $15-25

Equipment and Setup for Cat Grooming

You'll need some cat-specific equipment, though the investment is modest compared to the revenue potential.

Essential Equipment

  • Cat-specific restraint bag: Not a muzzle, but a mesh bag that allows grooming access while calming cats
  • Feline-appropriate dryer: Lower heat settings and quieter operation than dog dryers
  • Cat combs and brushes: Finer teeth and gentler bristles than dog grooming tools
  • Cat-safe shampoos: Formulated for feline skin pH, free of harmful ingredients
  • Guillotine-style nail clippers: Many groomers prefer these for cat nails over scissor-style
  • Small clipper blades: For detail work around faces and paws

Environment Considerations

Cats are stressed by dogs. Period. The sounds, smells, and energy of dogs in a grooming space trigger stress responses in most cats.

Options for addressing this:

  • Separate cat grooming space: Ideal but requires physical space
  • Dedicated cat grooming times: Early morning or late afternoon when no dogs are present
  • Sound barriers: Even a separate room with sound dampening helps
  • Pheromone diffusers: Feliway and similar products can reduce stress

Pricing Your Cat Grooming Services

Here's where many groomers make a mistake: they price cat grooming the same as or lower than dog grooming.

This is backwards.

Cat grooming requires specialized skills, carries higher risk, takes concentrated attention, and is scarce in the market. Your pricing should reflect this value.

Pricing Strategy

Pricing Principle: Your cat grooming prices should be 20-40% higher than equivalent dog services. The scarcity of qualified cat groomers justifies premium pricing, and cat owners are accustomed to paying more for specialized feline care.

Sample pricing structure:

  • Short-hair bath and groom: $65-85
  • Long-hair bath and groom: $85-110
  • De-matting (mild): Additional $20-40
  • De-matting (severe): Additional $50-100
  • Lion cut: $95-150
  • Comb cut: $85-120
  • Nail trim only: $15-25
  • Sanitary trim: $25-45

Always charge additional fees for:

  • Aggressive or extremely fearful cats requiring extra time
  • Severely matted coats
  • Medical conditions requiring extra care
  • Same-day or emergency appointments

Marketing to Cat Owners

Cat owners are a distinct demographic that requires targeted marketing approaches.

Where Cat Owners Are

  • Veterinary clinics: Leave cards and build referral relationships
  • Cat-specific social media groups: Facebook groups, Reddit communities, breed-specific forums
  • Pet supply stores: Particularly stores with strong cat product sections
  • Cat rescues and shelters: Offer discounted services for newly adopted cats to build relationships

Marketing Messages That Resonate

Cat owners want to know their pet will be:

  • Handled gently: Emphasize your gentle, patient approach
  • Kept separate from dogs: Highlight any dog-free accommodations
  • Groomed by someone who understands cats: Share your feline-specific training and experience
  • Returned calm: Cat owners dread picking up a traumatized pet

Content That Works

Create content around common cat owner concerns:

  • How to prevent matting between grooming appointments
  • Signs your cat needs professional grooming
  • What to expect during a cat grooming appointment
  • How to prepare your cat for their first groom
  • Before-and-after transformation photos (these perform incredibly well)

Managing Cat Grooming Services Efficiently

Adding cat services means managing a new service category with different scheduling needs, pricing structures, and client communication.

Scheduling Considerations

Cat appointments require different scheduling logic:

  • Buffer time: Cats may need settling time before grooming begins
  • Separate scheduling blocks: If you're creating dog-free times, your system needs to support this
  • Longer initial appointments: First-time cat clients need extra time for assessment
  • Flexibility: Some cats need breaks during grooming, extending appointment times

Client Information Tracking

Cat profiles need different information than dog profiles:

  • Handling preferences and sensitivities
  • Stress triggers and calming techniques that work
  • Medical conditions affecting grooming
  • Coat history (previous matting, lion cuts, etc.)
  • Behavioral notes for future appointments

Building Your Cat Clientele

Cat clients grow through reputation more than advertising. Focus on:

  • Exceptional first experiences: A calm, positive first groom creates a client for life
  • Post-appointment follow-up: Check in the next day to see how the cat is doing
  • Education: Teach owners how to maintain coats between visits
  • Referral incentives: Cat owners know other cat owners

The Bottom Line

Adding cat grooming services to your business isn't just about revenue, though the revenue is real. It's about positioning yourself as a complete pet care professional in an underserved market.

The groomers who develop feline expertise now will be the ones cat owners recommend for years to come. As cat ownership continues to grow and more owners recognize the need for professional grooming, early movers will have established reputations and loyal client bases.

Start with proper training. Invest in the right equipment. Create a cat-friendly environment. Price your services to reflect your specialized skills.

Then watch your appointment book, and your bank account, grow.
